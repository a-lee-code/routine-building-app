Hearth and Habit

Build sustainable habits and stay on track!


Hearth & Habit is designed with the ADHD brain in mind—my own included. But it’s not just for ADHDers; it’s for anyone who struggles with structure and routine.


Building a consistent and sustainable daily routine was the first step in managing my executive dysfunction, and I hope this tool helps you as much as it has helped me.


Let’s create habits that feel natural, achievable, and empowering—one step at a time.

Routines – Getting Started

Each routine “menu” provides dedicated spaces for your morning and evening routines. These blank spaces allow you to customize your habits based on what works best for you.


Not sure where to begin? A great starting point is choosing:

One habit for your mind (e.g., meditation)
One habit for your body (e.g., yoga)
One habit for joy (e.g., dancing to a favorite song)

Build a routine that feels natural, supportive, and sustainable for you!

Cleaning checklist - Getting started

The cleaning checklist has a few basic tasks added in and plenty of blank spaces for customization.


I encourage you to break tasks down into smaller, more manageable steps. Aim for small tasks that take no longer than 5 mins or less, it helps with sustainability.


For example, instead of listing "Clean the bathroom" as a single task, try breaking it down into individual steps like "Wipe the sink", "Scrub the toilet", or "Sweep the floor."


Start by adding one or two tasks to both your morning and evening routines—but remember, flexibility is key.

If you’re low on energy, it’s okay to skip a day. If you have extra energy, you can always do more. The goal is progress, not perfection!
